Range & Nut Advantage

Lesson 3 · Who should bet and why · 24 practice questions

Range advantage

The player whose range contains more strong hands on this board has range advantage. They can bet more often and at higher frequency.

Nut advantage

Even with range advantage, if villain holds more nutted combos (sets, straights), be careful — your value bets get called by better.

Practical takeaway

Preflop aggressor often has range advantage on high-card, dry flops. Caller has advantage on low, connected flops that hit their calling range.

Key takeaways

• Range advantage = more strong hands overall → bet more often.
• Nut advantage = more top combos → license to bet bigger.
• PFR is favored on high/dry flops; the caller catches up on low/connected flops.

FAQ

What is range advantage?

The player whose range holds more strong hands on a given board has range advantage, so they can bet more often and at higher frequency.

How is nut advantage different from range advantage?

Nut advantage is holding the very top of the range — sets, straights, nut flushes — more often than the opponent. It is what lets you use big bets and overbets credibly.
