Board Textures

Lesson 4 · Dry vs wet boards change strategy · 24 practice questions

Dry boards

Few draws, disconnected cards (K♠7♦2♣). Favors preflop raiser. High continuation-bet frequency.

Wet boards

Connected, flush draws (9♠8♠7♦). Favors caller's range. Check more, bet smaller.

Dynamic vs static

Dynamic boards change nuts often (draws complete). Static boards (paired, rainbow) stay stable — bet for value/protection early.

Key takeaways

• Dry/static boards: bet often and small — equities barely move.
• Wet/dynamic boards: bet selectively and bigger with value & draws.
• Match size and frequency to how often the nuts can change.

FAQ

What is board texture in poker?

Board texture describes how connected and coordinated the community cards are. Dry boards (like K-7-2 rainbow) favor the aggressor; wet, connected boards shift equity toward the caller.

Should you c-bet more on dry or wet boards?

You can c-bet a higher frequency on dry boards because your opponent connects less often; on wet boards you bet a tighter, more polarized range and size up.
