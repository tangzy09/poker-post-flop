Multi-Street Planning

Lesson 28 · Plan flop through river before you bet · 24 practice questions

Plan from the flop

Before betting, ask: which turns/rivers will I barrel? Which will I give up? Avoid betting with no plan.

Three-street value

Strong hands (sets, two pair+) plan to bet three streets. Size so you can bet river.

Give up lines

When the turn/river is bad for your range, check/give up. Don't barrel air on scare cards.

Key takeaways

• Plan flop → turn → river before betting.
• Strong hands: three-street value plan.
• Give up when board/runout favors villain.

Sample drill from this lesson

Flop, you hold set on 9-6-3. Plan three streets. Villain checks. Best action?

Board: 9♥ 6♦ 3♣
Your hand: 6♠ 6♥

Best action: Bet

Why: ✓ You have a set — bet for value and protection, charging worse hands and draws.

FAQ

What is multi-street planning?

Multi-street planning means deciding your line for the flop, turn, and river before you bet — which turn and river cards you’ll keep betting, which you’ll check, and how stacks get in.

Why plan the whole hand before betting the flop?

Betting without a plan leads to spots where you’ve bloated the pot with no good turn or river to continue. Planning ahead picks bets that set up profitable later streets.
