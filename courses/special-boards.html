Special Boards

Lesson 29 · Paired, monotone, and four-straight textures · 24 practice questions

Paired boards

Paired boards reduce straight/flush combos. Full houses and quads become possible. Bet less with one pair.

Monotone boards

Three of one suit — flush draws everywhere. Need a spade/heart/etc. to bet. Check more without one.

Dynamic boards

Connected, two-tone boards change nuts frequently. Size up with value and draws.

Key takeaways

• Paired: full houses possible, one pair weaker.
• Monotone: need suit to bet; check without.
• Dynamic: size up, plan for changing nuts.

FAQ

How do paired and monotone boards change strategy?

Paired boards reduce the number of strong hands, favoring aggression and thinner value; monotone boards let flushes appear, so the player with more of the top suit gets the nut advantage.

Should you bet more or less on scary boards?

It depends on who the texture favors. Bet more when the scare card is better for your range than your opponent’s; slow down when it hits the caller and caps your own range.
