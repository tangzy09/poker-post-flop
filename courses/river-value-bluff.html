River Value & Bluff Selection

Lesson 10 · Sizing value, picking bluffs · 24 practice questions

River value

Bet hands that beat >50% of villain's calling range. Size up vs inelastic callers (they call same with top pair or two pair).

Bluff selection

Bluff with blockers to nuts (e.g. Ah on three-heart board). Avoid bluffing into calling stations.

Over-bluffing loses

Each bluff needs enough fold equity. If villain's range is mostly strong, bluff less.

Key takeaways

• Thin value gets called by worse — size to target those hands.
• Pick bluffs from missed draws that block calls / unblock folds.
• Balance value-to-bluff to your bet size to stay unexploitable.

Sample drill from this lesson

River. You hold top two pair on a non-scary runout. Villain checks to you. Best action?

Best action: Bet

Why: ✓ You have two pair — bet for value and protection, charging worse hands and draws.
