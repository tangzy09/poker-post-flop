3-Bet Pot Postflop

Lesson 16 · Narrower ranges and lower SPR · 24 practice questions

Lower SPR by default

3-bet pots have smaller SPR (~4–6). Top pair+ is often committed; one pair alone may not be enough to stack off.

Range is tighter

Both players have stronger preflop ranges. C-bet less on low boards; value bet thinner on high boards.

Plan to stack off

In 3-bet pots, decide on the flop whether you're stacking off. Don't bet/call down with hands that can't stand a raise.

Key takeaways

• 3-bet pots = lower SPR, tighter ranges, faster commitment.
• Top pair+ often stacks off; medium pairs need caution.
• Size down on boards that miss your range; size up with range advantage.

FAQ

How does postflop play change in a 3-bet pot?

Ranges are narrower and stronger and the SPR is lower, so equities run closer and stacks go in faster. C-bets are frequent and top pair is often committed.

Should you c-bet more in single-raised or 3-bet pots?

The low SPR of a 3-bet pot supports frequent, often larger c-bets because both ranges are strong and there is less room to maneuver on later streets.
