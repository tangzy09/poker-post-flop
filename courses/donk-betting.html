Donk Betting

Lesson 21 · Leading OOP into the preflop raiser · 24 practice questions

When donking makes sense

Donk (lead) when the board favors your range more than the PFR's — e.g. low connected boards as the BB caller.

Donk for value

Lead with strong hands on boards that hit your calling range (sets, two pair on low boards).

Don't donk air

Leading with air on boards that favor the raiser is a leak — check and let them c-bet or check back.

Key takeaways

• Donk when the board favors your range over the PFR's.
• Lead for value with sets/two pair on low boards.
• Don't donk air on high/dry boards that favor the raiser.

FAQ

What is a donk bet?

A donk bet is a lead by the out-of-position player into the preflop aggressor, betting before giving them the chance to c-bet. It is used mainly on boards that hit the caller’s range harder.

Are donk bets a good idea?

Usually check to the aggressor, but a small donk lead is correct on boards that shift equity to the caller — low, connected flops the raiser misses often. Keep the range balanced with value and draws.
