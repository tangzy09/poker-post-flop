SPR & Commitment

Lesson 12 · Stack-to-pot ratio and stack-off decisions · 24 practice questions

SPR defined

SPR = effective stack / pot. Low SPR (<4): commit with top pair+. High SPR (>10): need stronger hands to stack off.

Commitment decisions

With SPR 2, pot-sized bets commit you. Plan stack-off ranges before betting — don't bet/fold incorrectly.

Tournament adjustment

Short tournament stacks create low SPR by default — tighten stack-off requirements.

Key takeaways

• Low SPR → commit with top pair+; high SPR → need a stronger hand to stack off.
• Plan stack-offs from the flop using SPR, not street by street.
• Don't bloat the pot with hands that can't stand a raise.
